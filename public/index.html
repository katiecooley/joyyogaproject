About the practice

I use a yoga framework to build somatic resourcing. Sessions develop capacity — through movement, breath, attention, visualization, therapeutic metaphor, archetype, and sacred geometry — to build the interior capacities that health depends on: interoception, proprioception, structural integrity, and nervous system regulation.

Classes are small and interactive.

People come to the work from chronic pain or long histories with yoga that have left them feeling like a tourist in their own body, or from athletic backgrounds where they have spent years training the body without ever fully inhabiting it.

This isn’t yoga as fitness, although it will build lung capacity, strength, and mobility. And it isn’t yoga as stress relief, although you will be able to hold more complexity and paradox. It’s closer to learning a language — one your body has been speaking all along.

On the name. Nevine Michaan, who developed Katonah Yoga®, teaches that when we face the direction of joy, the rearview mirror reveals everything that brought us here. The practice is in that spirit — oriented forward, with what came before integrated and visible behind.
